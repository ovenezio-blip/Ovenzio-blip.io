Poetry

I Am the River

I am the river, winding, restless, free,
I carry whispers of all who've touched me.
Feet that danced, hands that knelt,
Dreams that floated, hearts that felt.
I have known laughter, and sorrow too,
Silent tears and voices that flew.
Children who skipped stones across my face,
Lovers who lingered in my gentle embrace.

I remember the carts, the horses, the boats,
The fishermen's tales, the poets' notes.
Seasons have painted me green, gold, and gray,
Yet all who have crossed me have drifted away.

Still I flow, never still, never done,
Reflecting the sun, the moon, every one.
I hold their stories, their joy, their strife—
I am the river, the witness of life.

Short Story

The Diary's Warning

Alex found an old diary in the attic. Its pages predicted small events in town — but the last entry made their stomach drop: "On the third Friday of April, at the old bridge by Miller's Creek, disaster will strike."

The date was tomorrow. No one believed Alex, so they went alone. That night, flashlight in hand, they crept to the bridge. A rotten support loomed beneath, barely holding.

Heart hammering, Alex reinforced it with rope and spare planks — but just as they finished, a delivery truck rumbled toward the bridge. The wood groaned and cracked. Alex froze.

With one last desperate shove, they tightened the rope — and the truck crossed, shaking the bridge but leaving it intact. Alex collapsed, trembling. They had saved the day, but barely.

As they returned the diary to the attic, Alex realized some warnings weren't meant to be ignored… and some thrills weren't meant to be survived without a fight.
